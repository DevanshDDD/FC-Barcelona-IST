Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi, who is regarded as one of the all-time greats in football, has a spectacular history with FC Barcelona. Messi, who was up in Rosario, Argentina, enrolled in Barcelona's La Masia youth academy when he was 13 years old. He advanced fast through the ranks, making his first-team debut in 2004 when he was just 17 years old. Since then, Messi has played his whole professional career for Barcelona, compiling an incredible resume. With the club, he has won many national and international championships, including four Champions League titles, seven Copa del Rey medals, and ten La Liga championships. As the club's all-time leading scorer and the player with the most goals in a single La Liga season, Messi has set countless more records. He has become a cherished character among Barça supporters thanks to his extraordinary talent and dedication to the team; many believe he is the best player in the club's history. Messi, however, departed the team in August 2021 due to financial difficulties and joined Paris Saint-Germain.

Joan Laporta

As the club's president on two distinct occasions, Joan Laporta is a significant figure in FC Barcelona's past. Laporta was originally elected club president in 2003, and throughout his seven-year term, he presided over one of the club's most prosperous eras. Barca won multiple national and international championships under Laporta's management, including four La Liga championships and two Champions League crowns. Some of the club's most known players, like Ronaldinho and Samuel Eto'o, were recruited by Laporta. In 2021, Laporta was re-elected as club president after a time of discontent and uncertainty. Fans and players alike have welcomed his return with hope, hoping that he can help the team reclaim its previous glory. Laporta is renowned for his dedication to the Catalan identity of the team and his advocacy for charitable causes. His management style has been characterised as charismatic and imaginative, and he is recognised as one of the most successful and significant club presidents in history.

Johan Cruyff

The influence Johan Cruyff had on FC Barcelona and football in general is immense. When Cruyff became a player for Barca in 1973, it changed the course of the team's history. He contributed to changing the team's playing style by bringing a more offensive and possession-focused strategy that would later be dubbed "Total Football." In 1974, Barça won its first La Liga championship under Cruyff, and he was voted European Footballer of the Year. Cruyff returned to Barca as a coach in 1988 when his playing career was over, continuing to alter the team's method of play. He established the "Dream Team" period, during which Barça won its first-ever European Cup and four straight La Liga championships. Cruyff had an impact on Barcelona that went beyond the playing field. He helped establish La Masia, the club's youth academy, which produced some of the best players in football history, including Lionel Messi and Xavi. Cruyff is regarded as one of the most significant personalities in the history of Barca and his influence there is still felt today.

Xavi Hernandez

Many people consider Xavi Hernandez to be one of FC Barcelona's all-time great players. At the age of 11, he enrolled in the club's La Masia youth academy and rapidly worked his way up to the first team. Throughout his 17-year career with Barcelona, Xavi won numerous national and international championships, including eight La Liga championships, four Champions League crowns, and two FIFA Club World Cups. Xavi's playing style, which prioritised rapid passes and perfect ball control, became associated with Barca, and he played a crucial role in the team's success in the late 2000s and early 2010s. After Carles Puyol left Barça in 2014, Xavi was named captain because of his team's great regard for his leadership abilities. Xavi is recognised as one of the best midfielders in football history because of his dedication to Barça's guiding principles and his unshakable allegiance to the team. After ending his playing career, Xavi returned to Barça as a coach and is expected to oversee the team in the future.
